- Economy metaphor: money as water — design faucets (income) and sinks (costs/upkeep) to create meaningful choices.
- Pricing used an exponential value-scaling formula to reward deeper production chains rather than raw volume.
- Common traps: infinite wallet (no sinks), linear progression (checklist upgrades), optimal path dominance, and lack of clear feedback for players.
- Good economies create tension via tradeoffs, are understandable, fair, and let players shape outcomes.
